## Core Idea

**Summary:**

Humble and Farley argue that releasing software reliably and frequently depends on automation, fast feedback, and a culture of continuous improvement.

Continuous delivery reduces risk by making each release small, tested, and deployable at any time.

---

## Release Principles

- Keep the build fast and automated.
- Deploy to production-like environments.
- Automate tests and acceptance criteria.
- Make deployment routine, not risky.

---

## Practical Checklist

- [ ] Automate your build and deployment process.
- [ ] Run tests in an environment similar to production.
- [ ] Keep deploys small and incremental.
- [ ] Monitor releases and fix fast.

## Notes

The most practical lesson is that fast delivery depends on treating release as a routine operational activity, not a dramatic event.
