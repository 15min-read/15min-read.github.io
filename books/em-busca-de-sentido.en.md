## Core Lesson

**Summary:**

Viktor Frankl argues that meaning is the central human motivation. Even in extreme suffering, people can preserve freedom by finding purpose in their experience.

Frankl draws on his time in concentration camps to show that those who survived often held onto a sense of mission, a future goal, or an inner attitude that meaning mattered.

---

## Meaning in Hardship

- Suffering can be transformed by the attitude we choose.
- A meaningful task or vision helps maintain inner freedom.
- Love, work, and courage are sources of meaning.

The most powerful moment is the reminder that meaning is chosen, not given. This book is often read as a guide to resilience and inner strength.
