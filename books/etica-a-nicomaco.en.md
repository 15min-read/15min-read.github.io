## Core Thesis

**Summary:**

Aristotle describes the good life as the result of practicing virtues consistently. Ethics is not only theory; it is a habit formed by repeated action.

Virtue sits between excess and deficiency: courage lies between recklessness and cowardice, generosity between wastefulness and stinginess.

---

## Virtue as Practice

- Ethical behavior is learned through practice.
- Good choices build character over time.
- The aim is eudaimonia — flourishing through purposeful living.
