## Core Claim

**Summary:**

Daniel Goleman argues that emotional intelligence (EQ) is as important as IQ for success. EQ includes self-awareness, self-regulation, empathy, and social skills.

People with high EQ can manage stress, build relationships, and make better decisions. These abilities often determine performance in leadership and collaboration.

---

## Emotional Competencies

- Self-awareness: understanding your emotions.
- Self-regulation: controlling impulses.
- Motivation: staying driven.
- Empathy: reading others.
- Social skill: navigating relationships.

---

## Practical Checklist

- [ ] Pause before reacting to strong emotions.
- [ ] Practice active listening with others.
- [ ] Notice patterns in your moods.
- [ ] Use empathy to consider a colleague's perspective.
- [ ] Develop small habits for emotional balance.

## Notes

The most useful idea is that emotional skills are learnable and that they matter across work, relationships, and leadership.
