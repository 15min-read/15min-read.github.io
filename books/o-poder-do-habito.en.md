## Core Idea

**Summary:**

Charles Duhigg explains that habits are built from a loop of cue, routine, and reward. Understanding this loop makes it possible to change habits and build new ones.

The book emphasizes that small changes in routine can dramatically alter everyday behavior, especially when the cue and reward are kept constant.

---

## Habit Loop

- Cue: a trigger that starts the behavior.
- Routine: the behavior itself.
- Reward: the benefit your brain receives.

By identifying the cue and reward, you can substitute a new routine and reshape the habit loop.

---

## Key Rules

- Habits are not destiny; they can be changed.
- Belief is essential for lasting change.
- Keystone habits can create ripple effects across life.

---

## Implementation Checklist

- [ ] Identify a habit loop in your daily life.
- [ ] Define the cue and reward clearly.
- [ ] Replace the routine with a better action.
- [ ] Use small wins to build belief.
- [ ] Track progress and adjust when needed.
